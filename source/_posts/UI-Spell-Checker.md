<p style="color:#aaa;">Master Project | Year: 2017</p>

Have you ever thought about spell checking system continues to interrupt typing flow? Sometimes I just want to correct all my spelling mistakes at one time after my paragraph comes to an end. However the spell checker will pop out to show some spelling suggestions or just replace my mistakes automatically while I am still typing.

Watch prototype vedio here: **[Demo link](https://youtu.be/OZd7z146puE)**

**Took inspiration from coin pusher:**

<div style="text-align:center;margin-bottom:40px;">
<img src="https://drive.google.com/uc?export=view&id=1k0QFWH8i5AD2awNhJ0gmNY-WPujawNQ4" width="250px" height="auto" />
</div>

My idea is, spelling mistakes in article will be remarked as a solid(3D) materials. When one tap on the trigger for spell check. It will act like a coin pusher to push spelling mistakes to another layer. So one has an independent and clean layer for spell checking. After finish the spell checking, one can tap on the trigger again to push the corrections back to original layer.

In design version 2 and 3, I thought deeper about various situations. For example, errors happen in close positions. When swipe to another page, how do they show and expand afterward.
Also play with font size, position and animation that clearly show users where their mistakes lay. Emphasize the comparison between wrong word and corrected word, which help to enhance learning process and hopefully reduce spelling errors.
